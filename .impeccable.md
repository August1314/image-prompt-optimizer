## Design Context

### Users
Image Prompt Optimizer serves creators and solo operators who use AI image tools to produce marketing visuals, thumbnails, social assets, concept frames, and product imagery. They usually arrive with a rough idea, not a polished prompt, and want a workflow that helps them clarify and refine quickly without feeling technical or overwhelming.

### Brand Personality
Calm, premium, precise.

The interface should feel trustworthy and mature rather than flashy. It should evoke confidence, editorial polish, and product quality. The tone should communicate that this is a serious software product with thoughtful UX, not a novelty AI experiment.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should feel like a high-end software website with a restrained editorial sensibility. Keep the existing light-mode foundation and avoid drifting into template-site sameness, AI-demo theatrics, or e-commerce detail-page patterns. Images should be used as proof and atmosphere, not as decorative noise. Typography, spacing, and hierarchy should lead the page; visuals should support that structure while preserving original image aspect ratios.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with software credibility. Every section should read like a premium product page, not a design gallery or trend-driven landing page.
2. Preserve image integrity. Marketing visuals must keep their original aspect ratios; improve composition through framing, scale, alignment, spacing, and sequencing instead of cropping tricks.
3. Use editorial restraint. Favor strong hierarchy, generous whitespace, and a small number of confident layout moves over dense card walls or noisy decoration.
4. Keep the page commercially legible. The user should understand what the product does, why it matters, and why it feels trustworthy within the first screen and a half.
5. Avoid generic AI aesthetics. No neon-hacker clichés, no over-animated showcase patterns, and no marketplace-style product merchandising.
